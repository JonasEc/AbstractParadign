Of the class of {{Constants.NumPeople}} in the class, one student was randomly selected: call him or her student X.

At the end of the experiment, you will guess what letter grade this student X received.

You will receive information about student X’s performance on the test relative to his or her classmates. Specifically, in each of {{Constants.num_rounds}} rounds, another classmate will be picked at random and you will learn whether student X performed better or worse than this classmate on the test.

For every round, there is an equal probability that any of the classmates is picked. It is hence possible that the same classmate is picked in more than one round.

In each round, you will also be paid to guess student X’s precise ranking (where 1 means best and {{Constants.NumPeople}} means worst of the class).
